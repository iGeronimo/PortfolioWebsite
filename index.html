This is a space tycoon game that was made as part of my exchange program with OAMK, a Finnish university. In this project I was mainly tasked with the space travel component. In the game, when you go on a space expedition, my code will generate a universe full of planets with different resources, danger levels and travel risks.
It was was one of the more challenging and interesting projects I have worked on.

We published the game on Google Playstore at the end of the exchange program and had a period in which we advertised the game through google ads.
The universe generation works through first spawning planets layer by layer. It goes through several checks of distance, angle and proximity. When all layers of planets have been completed the next step is to draw lines between all the planets. The first set of lines is drawn from the last layer to its closest neighbour in an earlier layer. Repeating this process ensures that all furthest planets are reachable. After this it is a reverse process of first layer to closest in second layer (not a repeating an earlier connection). And then lastly, connecting the planets within their own layer. All lines are checked to not intersect with one another and in the image you can see the result. I'd like to revisit this script in the future and make it more evenly spread and with better performance.

Height Mapping Terrain
Tags: C++, OpenGL, Shaders
A flat plane terrain mesh altered through the use of a black to white colored height map. After this the normals are recalculated through the adjacent vertexes heights and some lighting is added to make it seem a little more realistic. It was made with OpenGL, C++.
Ray Path Tracing
Tags: C#, Unity, Shaders
After trying out lighting a little bit with the height mapping in OpenGL I wanted to take it a step further in terms of realism. This scene was made in Unity and uses Path tracing to determine light reflections more accurately. In this image it shows up to 10 bounces of the light hitting any surface.
